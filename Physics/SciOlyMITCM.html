Problem 6 (6 pts)

Many odern vinyl turntables are belt drive; a motor with a small pulley is coupled to a much larger record platter with a belt. For the audio to have the right tempo and pitch, it's critical that the platter spins at \(33\frac{1}{3}\) rpm (for most records).

Suppose a turntable has a platter pulley radius of 12.0 cm and its drive pulley has a radius of 1.00 cm. Calculate:

  1. The speed in rpm that the motor must rotate at.
  2. The mechanical advantage of the belt system.
  3. The required torque of the motor given that the belt must apply 2.00N of force to the platter for it to spin at a constant \(33\frac{1}{3}\) rpm.

Solution:

Part 1:

It is crucial to find out what is being conserved here, and the quantity I used was translational displacement along the belt. Given a set amount of time, say 1 minute, the distance covered by the rotation of the platter must be covered by the rotation of the motor drive. So we first find the translational displacement (\(d_T\)):

\[ d_T = 2 \pi (12.0 cm) \times \frac{100}{3} rpm \times 1 min \approx 2510 cm \]

We know that the motor drive, rotating at \(\omega_m\) need to cover 2510 cm within 1 minute, so we get the following equation:

\[ 2510 cm = 2 \pi (1.00 cm) \times \omega_m \times 1 min \] \[ \boxed{\omega_m = 400. rpm} \]

Part 2:

This solution assumes that you do not know the formula for the IMA of a belt system. If you do the problem becomes trivial.

We need to figure out a formula for the IMA of a belt system, the process is quite simple really. Since the work done by both are the same, the torque applied must be equal, so we get:

\[ R_e \times F_e = R_r \times F_r \]

So we get that:

\[ IMA = \frac{F_r}{F_e} = \frac{R_r}{R_e} = \frac{12.0 cm}{1.00 cm} = \boxed{12} \]

Part 3:

This is just straight forward calculation:

\[ \tau = F \times R = 2.00N (1.00 cm) = \boxed{0.0200 Nm} \]

Problem 8 (7 pts)

Your Bungee Drop teammates need help! They ask you to devise a clamp to anchor their bungee device that can be hand-tightened to the correct pressure for maximum convenience. They've determined that the clamp must exert 210. N of force on the bungee device to hold it and request that it can be tightened with only 5.00 N of effort. The clamp will use a lever coupled to a screw, similar to many vises. The only screw available for the clamp has an outside diameter of 12.0mm and a pitch of 2.0mm. Calculate the minimum lever length needed to satisfy their requirements.

So bascially, your team mate want a wheel-axel-screen system that will amplify a 5.00 N force to a 210. N force. So the IMA of said machine is 42.0

The screw part of the system has a mechanical advantage of \(\frac{(12.0 mm) \pi}{2.00 mm} = 18.8\). This means that the wheel-axel system need to have an IMA of \(42.0 \div 18.8 = 2.23\) to make this work.

So, we just have to solve for \(R_w\):

\[ \frac{R_w}{12.0mm \div 2} = 2.23 \] \[ R_w = 2.23 \times 6.00 mm = \boxed{13.4 mm} \]
