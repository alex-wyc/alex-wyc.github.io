Station 1 - Thermodynamics

1. What is Newton’s Law of Cooling? How does the experiment you ran in station 1 affected or modeled by this law? What are the differences between this law and your best fit model, and which more accurately models this rate of cooling? (10 points)

Answer:

Newton's Law of Cooling:

\[ T(t) = T_a + (T_i - T_a)e^{-kt} \]

It states that there is a negative exponential relationship between the temperature and time of an object under the influence of the surrounding. In the experiment we just conducted,there was a change in temperature within the water due to external influence, and therefore can be modelled using Newton's Law of cooling, we can find K using the measurements and use it to predict future changes in temperature. However, the law and the experiment are not completely the same in that Newton's Law of Cooling assumes a static environment with no outside influence except the difference in temperature, which is not the case here. However, in the long run, Newton's Law of Cooling is still the preferred model in that the experimental measure (almost linear) relies severely on the sample size, which is pretty small in this case.

Newton's Law of Cooling has a lot of limitations which causes errors when applied to real world scenario.
