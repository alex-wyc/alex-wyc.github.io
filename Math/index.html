2015-01-02

Algebra Problem: If \(x_1, x_2, x_3 ... x_n\) are positive, find with proof the minimum value of: \[ (x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + ... + x_n)(\frac{1}{x_1} + \frac{1}{x_2} + \frac{1}{x_3} + ... + \frac{1}{x_n}) \]

Problem from "Math Circle for Olympians" by Henryk Pawlowski

Hint: Use Rearrangement

Solution:

WLOG, we can assume that \(x_1 \geq x_2 \geq x_3 \geq ... \geq x_n\). Therefore, we also know that \(\frac{1}{x_n} \geq \frac{1}{x_{n-1}} \geq \frac{1}{x_{n-2}} \geq ... \geq \frac{1}{x_1}\).

From the rearrangement inequality, we know that the minimum value of any linear combination of these two sequences is when the order of the two sequences completely mismatches. Therefore the minimum value of any linear combination is \(\frac{1}{x_n} \cdot x_n + \frac{1}{x_{n-1}} \cdot x_{n-1} + \frac{1}{x_{n-2}} \cdot x_{n-2} + ... + \frac{1}{x_2} \cdot x_2 + \frac{1}{x_1} \cdot x_1\), which will give you n.

Now let's expand the left hand side: \[x_1 \cdot \frac{1}{x_1} + x_2 \cdot \frac{1}{x_2} + ... + x_n \cdot \frac{1}{x_n} +\] \[x_1 \cdot \frac{1}{x_2} + x_2 \cdot \frac{1}{x_3} + ... + x_n \cdot \frac{1}{x_1} +\] \[... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ... ...\] \[x_1 \cdot \frac{1}{x_{n-1}} + x_2 \cdot \frac{1}{x_n} + ... + x_n \cdot \frac{1}{x_{n-2}} +\] \[x_1 \cdot \frac{1}{x_n} + x_2 \cdot \frac{1}{x_1} + ... + x_n \cdot \frac{1}{x_{n-1}}\]

Now we apply rearrangement to each "row" of the expansion. Notice hat each row is greater than or equal to n, and since there are n rows, their sum is greater than or equal to \(n \times n\), or \(\boxed{n^2}\blacksquare\)


Bonus Solution by Brandon Lin:

We create two vectors, \(\langle \sqrt{x_1}, \sqrt{x_2}, \sqrt{x_3}, ... , \sqrt{x_n} \rangle\) and \(\langle \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_1}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_2}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_3}}, ... , \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_n}} \rangle\).

Now we apply the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality, we get the following: \[ (x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + ... + x_n)(\frac{1}{x_1} + \frac{1}{x_2} + \frac{1}{x_3} + ... + \frac{1}{x_n}) \geq \] \[ (\frac{1}{\sqrt{x_1}} \cdot \sqrt{x_1} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_2}} \cdot \sqrt{x_2} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_3}} \cdot \sqrt{x_3} + ... + \frac{1}{\sqrt{x_n}} \cdot \sqrt{x_n})^2 \]

The right hand side simplifies to \(\boxed{n^2}\blacksquare\)
