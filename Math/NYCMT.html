1. If (319, b, 481) is a Pythagorean triple, with b < 481, find b.

Solution:

At first glance the numbers may look intimidating, but a little algebra makes our lives much easier. By the Pythagorean theorem we get: \[ 319^2 + b^2 = 481^2 \] \[ b^2 = 481^2 - 319^2 = (481 + 319)(481 - 319) = 8 \times 100 \times 2 \times 81 \] \[ \boxed{b = 4 \times 10 \times 9 = 360} \blacksquare \]

2. The hypotenuse of a right triangle is 11 and its area is 21. Find its perimeter

Solution:

Let the two legs of the rigth triangle have lengths \(a\) and \(b\) respectively, and let \(c = 11\). By the Pythagorean theorem we have: \[ a^2 + b^2 = 11^2 = 121 \]

By the area formula of a triangle, we have: \[ \frac{1}{2}ab = 21 \] \[ \therefore 2ab = 84 \]

Add the two together: \[ a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a + b)^2 = 121 + 84 = 205 a + b = \sqrt{205} \] \[ \therefore \boxed{a + b + c = 11 + \sqrt{205}} \]

3. On the coordinate plane: A(12, 20), B(57, 100), C(-30, -63). Find the coordinates of the intersection of the medians of \(\triangle\)ABC.

Solution:

The intersection of the medians is the centroid, or the center of mass of the triangle, its coordinates is therefore \((\bar{x}, \bar{y})\).

Therefore its x and y values are: \[ x = \frac{12 + 57 - 30}{3} = 4 + 19 - 10 = 13 \] \[ y = \frac{20 + 100 - 63}{3} = 40 - 21 = 19 \]

Therefore the coordinates of the centroid is \(\boxed{(13, 19)} \blacksquare\)

4. A class with 11 girls and \(n\) boys visited a library. Eachchild checked out the same number of books. Together they checked out \(n^2 + 9n - 2\) books from the library. How many boys are there in the class?

Solution:

Let the number of books each person checked out be \(a\). So we know that \[ a(11 + n) = n^2 + 9n - 2 \]

Divide by \(11 + n\), we get: \[ a = \frac{n^2 + 9n - 2}{n + 11} = n - 2 + \frac{20}{n + 11} \]

We know that a has to be an integer, that means \(n + 11\) must be a divisor of 20. That forces \(\boxed{n = 9} \blacksquare\)

5. In \(\triangle ABC\), \(P\) is on \(\overline{AB}\), and \(AP:PB = 1:3\). The line through \(P\) and the centroid of \(\triangle ABC\) divides \(\overline{BC}\) at point Q. Find \(BQ:QC\)

Solution:

Triangle, centroid, ratios, chevians? This problem screams MASS POINTS

Refer to the above diagram, we assign mass 3 to point A. Since D is the midpoint of \(\overline{AC}\), we also assign a mass of 3 to point C, that means we assign a mass 6 to point D. Since the centroid divides the median in a 1:2 ratio, we assign a mass of 3 to point B. However, P divides \(\overline{AB}\) into a 1:3 ratio, that means the split mass of B on this side is 1, so the split mass on the other side is 2. Therefore, \(\boxed{BQ:QC = 3:2} \blacksquare\)
